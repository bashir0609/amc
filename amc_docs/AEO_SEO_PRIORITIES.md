# AEO & SEO Implementation Priorities

This document outlines the highest priority actionable items to optimize the Auto MOT Centre website for Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) and Search Engine Optimization (SEO).

## High Priority: Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)

AEO focuses on appearing in answers generated by AI tools like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Perplexity. They prioritize factual, structured, and easily digestible information.

1.  **Structured Data (JSON-LD Schema)**
    - **Action:** Add `LocalBusiness` or `AutoRepair` schema to the site (ideally on the homepage and contact page).
    - **Details:** Include business name, address (Manor Park, London), phone number, opening hours, services offered, and price range.
    - **Action:** Add `FAQPage` schema.
    - **Details:** Create and markup a Frequently Asked Questions section (e.g., "What does a Class 4 MOT include?", "How much does a car service cost?").

2.  **Conversational Q&A Content**
    - **Action:** Restructure content to directly answer user questions.
    - **Details:** Use headings formatted as questions and follow up immediately with clear paragraphs or bulleted lists.

3.  **Clear Authority Signals (E-E-A-T)**
    - **Action:** Highlight Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness.
    - **Details:** Showcase customer reviews/testimonials prominently, ensure contact information is consistent everywhere, and add an "About Us" section highlighting mechanic experience.

## High Priority: Search Engine Optimization (SEO)

SEO focuses on ranking high in traditional search engine results (like Google Search).

1.  **Sitemap & Robots.txt**
    - **Action:** Generate `app/sitemap.ts` and `app/robots.ts`.
    - **Details:** Help search engines systematically discover all pages on your site.

2.  **Page-Specific Metadata**
    - **Action:** Export `metadata` objects in each specific `page.tsx` file (e.g., `/services`, `/contact-us`, `/mot-booking`).
    - **Details:** Customize `<title>` and `<meta name="description">` to accurately reflect the content of _that specific page_, rather than just relying on the global layout metadata.

3.  **Semantic HTML & Accessibility**
    - **Action:** Enforce a strict heading hierarchy.
    - **Details:** Ensure only one `<h1>` per page, followed logically by `<h2>` and `<h3>` tags.
    - **Action:** Add descriptive `alt` tags to all images (specifically using `next/image`).

## Next Steps

To begin, we should tackle **Structured Data (JSON-LD)** and **Page-Specific Metadata**, as these offer the highest immediate value for both AI comprehension and traditional search rankings.
